The Year That
Changed Everything
and the person who made it bearable
The beginning
12th grade was a lot. Chaotic in the way that only that particular year can be — the kind of overwhelming that sneaks up on you slowly and then hits all at once. The pressure, the noise, the uncertainty of what comes next. Nobody really tells you how hard it can get.
✦
The hard part
There were things that year that were genuinely difficult. The kind of hardship that makes you feel a little more alone than usual — even when you're surrounded by people. But somewhere in the middle of all of it, there was you. You didn't fix anything, even I couldn't — some things just have to be lived through. But you stayed. You listened. You showed up with the kind of quiet, steady presence that only the right people know how to give.
✦
What you did
You listened when I needed to talk. You sat with me when I didn't. You gave honest advice when I needed direction and just presence when I didn't need anything else. That's a rare thing. Most people don't know how to do that. You did it naturally — like it was the most obvious thing in the world. Because for you, it was. That's just who you are.
✦
The moment I knew
That's when I knew. Not when we met — friendships don't really begin at hellos. They begin in the moments that test them. And you passed every single one without even trying. 12th grade was the year a lot of things fell apart. But it was also the year I found out exactly who my people are. You are at the top of that list. You have always been at the top of that list.
✦
After
School ended. Different colleges, same city — but somehow life got busier and the distance felt bigger than the kilometres. The kind of friendship that could have quietly faded. And here we are — five years later — and you are still the person I call. Still the one who listens and advices. Still the one who shows up. Still you.
5 years & counting
You walked into my life as a classmate.
You stayed as something so much more than that.
